Abstract 

In response to environmental and inflammatory cues, microglia adopt diverse morphologies reflec=ng their func=onal state. However, characterizing these morphological altera=ons in human iPSC-derived microglia (iMGLs) remains limited by reliance on fluorescent labeling, endpoint imaging, and coarse categorical classifica=ons (e.g., ramified vs amoeboid states). Here, we developed a label-free pipeline combining live-cell imaging, Cellpose-SAM segmenta=on, and CellProfiler-based feature extrac=on to track iMGL morphology at single-cell resolu=on over time . Applying this framework to a 24-hour =me course of LPS and IFNγ co-s=mula=on revealed rapid and transient morphological remodeling, with responses peaking within 2–4 hours and par=ally adenua=ng thereaeer. At single-cell resolu=on, four morphological states captured the major axes of varia=on, with co-s=mula=on driving a pronounced redistribu=on toward a single “Spread state”. Single-cell pre-processing. Several limita=ons should be considered. First, morphological features provide an indirect readout of cellular state and do not directly capture the underlying molecular programs.
