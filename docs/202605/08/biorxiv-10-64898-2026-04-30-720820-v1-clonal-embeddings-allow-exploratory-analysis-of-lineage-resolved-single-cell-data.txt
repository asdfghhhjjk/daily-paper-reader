Abstract 16 

> 17

Assays coupling high-throughput lineage tracing with single-cell transcriptomics are transforming 18 

studies of development and disease biology, revealing not only major differentiation routes but 19 

also continuous fate biases and their putative regulators. Yet, analysis of such data at scale 20 

presents challenges due to the sparse nature of clonal data and annotation dependencies. 21 

Towards that aim we developed a machine learning approach – clone2vec – which learns 22 

informative clone embeddings directly from the cellular expression manifold, bypassing discrete 23 

cell-type labels and remaining stable when clones are represented by few cells. This 24 

representation summarizes clonal variation as an interpretable geometry that supports 25 

exploration, statistics for clone-gene associations, and cross-dataset alignment. In prospective 26 

barcoding datasets spanning embryogenesis, tumorigenesis, and hematopoiesis, clone2vec 27 

recapitulates established clonal patterns and uncovers new axes of continuous variation that 28 

implicate regulatory programs and developmental pathways. In tumor microenvironments profiled 29 

with TCR sequencing, clone2vec robustly recovers distinct Treg lineages as well as conserved 30 

CD8 + T cell sublineages across cancer types, including several bystander-like clonal subsets. 31 

Overall, clone2vec provides a robust, general solution for the exploratory analysis of lineage-32 

coupled scRNA-seq data. 33

> 35

Main 36 

> 37

Arising from a single cell, multicellular organisms develop through clonal expansion and fate 38 

specification to create, maintain, and regenerate tissues. The gradual diversification allows initially 39 

identical progenitors to explore vast phenotypic spaces. Consequently, the value of exploring 40 

clonal variation – whether in morphogenesis, stem cell niche dynamics, or immune responses – 41 

lies in its ability to reveal the specific intrinsic and extrinsic factors that shape variability in lineage 42 

behavior. Lineage variability is not merely developmental noise, but a functional property of living 43 

systems. It reflects how individual progenitors distribute potential across space and time, maintain 44

Clonal diversity is also important for functional and developmental tissue robustness. It is 46 

obviously central to the function of the adaptive immune system. In development, clonal 47 

heterogeneity can buffer perturbations by allowing some clones to compensate for local losses 1,2 ,48 

expand under stress 3,4 , or adjust outputs in response to neighboring deficits 5,6 . Similar clones may 49 

diverge in different anatomical or signaling environments, while distinct clones may converge 50 

within the same niche 7,8 . Clonal analysis can therefore help to disentangle the interplay between 51 

ancestry and position, especially in migratory and multipotent systems shaped by changing local 52 

contexts. 53 

> 54

Indeed, in developmental biology, lineage tracing can reveal spatial regulatory boundaries 55 

invisible to standard anatomical assessment. For example, in the murine hair follicle, tracing has 56 

shown that a stem cell’s initial spatial position – in the niche bulge or hair germ – is the primary 57 

predictor of its fate 9, with local niche cues actively reprogramming cells to stem-like or 58 

differentiated states. Similarly, in vertebrate limb mesenchyme, genetic labeling uncovered a 59 

sharp dorsoventral lineage restriction plane that does not coincide with morphological 60 

boundaries 10 , indicating that early positional signals imprint progenitors with regional identities 61 

that are maintained in the subsequent clonal expansion. Clonal analysis has also revealed many 62 

contexts in which fate choice is strongly influenced by cell-intrinsic priming, often by exploiting the 63 

shared ancestry of sister cells. In the hematopoietic system, for example, lineage tracing has 64 

demonstrated that stem cells (HSCs) belonging to the same clone exhibit highly stereotypic 65 

behaviors, including sustained lineage bias and specific stress responses 11 . This cell-intrinsic 66 

programming is sufficiently stable that clonally related cells maintain their distinctive biases even 67 

when separated and transplanted into independent recipients, with their clonal biases overriding 68 

the influence of local microenvironments 11,12 . As well, lineage tracing also allows researchers to 69 

identify cases when this intrinsic similarity is overridden by extrinsic signals. In the CD8 + T cell 70 

compartment, sisters derived from a single naive progenitor can diverge dramatically, generating 71 

both short-lived effectors and long-lived memory cells, driven by asymmetric exposure to antigen 72 

or polarity cues during the initial division 13–16 . Finally, the similarity of clonally related sister cells 73 

has also been used to overcome the fundamental limitation of the destructive genomic assays – 74 

their inability to measure the same cell at multiple points in time. By using clonally-traced sister 75 

cells as proxies for earlier molecular states, researchers have retrospectively approximated 76 

cellular trajectories during complex transitions such as in vitro reprogramming and drug resistance 77 

evolution 17–20 .78 

> 79

To capture clonal dynamics, lineage tracing techniques have evolved significantly from early dyes 80 

and single-gene reporters. The introduction of multicolor recombinase systems, such as 81 

Brainbow, expanded the resolution of in vivo tracing 21,22 , though spectral overlap limited the 82 

number of distinct clones that could be reliably identified. This limitation was overcome by high-83 

throughput genetic barcoding schemes using site-specific recombination or viral libraries, which 84 

allow for the tracking of thousands of clones to estimate size distributions and lineage biases 23,24 .85 

More recently, CRISPR “scarring” techniques have enabled the reconstruction of complex lineage 86 

trees by accumulating dynamic edits over time, providing an organism-scale view of clonal 87 

heterogeneity 8,25 . Crucially, the combination of these technologies with single-cell transcriptome 88

A number of computational tools has been developed to interrogate lineage-coupled scRNA-seq 92 

data in different ways. These include, for example, methods for tree reconstruction 26,27 , inference 93 

of early progenitor biasing 28,29 , or lineage-informed cell clustering 30 . A common initial step in 94 

analysis of such data, however, is to characterize and navigate the overall diversity of the 95 

observed clonal behaviors. This crucial step presents its own computational challenges. Most 96 

clones are represented by very few captured cells due to technical undersampling and biological 97 

stochasticity, resulting in data sparsity and overdispersion that make the interpretation of 98 

individual clonal events unreliable. Heavy-tailed clone-size distributions confound distance 99 

measures of clonal similarity. Additional complications arise from convergent edits or homoplasy 100 

and barcode collisions in CRISPR-based systems. As a result, clone-level summaries based on 101 

cell-type proportions or hierarchical clustering can be unstable and sensitive to the exact cell 102 

annotation. Recent methods now yield thousands of clones, making it possible to capture rich 103 

clonal variation and probe the underlying developmental logic. However, robust frameworks for 104 

large-scale clonal classification and typology – especially in systems where clonal architectures 105 

vary dramatically and continually – are still lacking. Exploration and analysis of such data, 106 

therefore, necessitates new scalable approaches to quantifying clonal variation and dynamics on 107 

the expression manifolds. 108 

> 109

Furthermore, a cell type-centric perspective, typically used in exploratory analysis, can fail to 110 

capture the full spectrum of clonal variation in the data. First, the resolution and structure of cell 111 

type annotations may be insufficient. In the murine in vitro hematopoiesis system 19 , for example, 112 

functionally distinct monocyte lineages occupy transcriptionally separate monocyte states that are 113 

nevertheless collapsed into a single “monocyte” category when aggregating cells by expression 114 

similarity alone (Supplementary Fig. 1a,b). Even at increasing resolution, the annotation 115 

boundaries may be ill-defined. In tumor-infiltrating CD8 + T cell compartments 31 , for example, 116 

phenotypic states such as tissue-resident memory, effector, and transitional populations form 117 

continuous gradients rather than discrete clusters (Supplementary Fig. 1c,d). Clustering of such 118 

data is likely to produce arbitrary borders, which may distort or obscure clonal variation patterns 32 .119 

A cluster-free approach is therefore needed to quantify similarities between clonal distributions in 120 

an unbiased manner. 121 

> 122

Here we introduce clone2vec, a robust, annotation-free approach for constructing an effective 123 

overview of clonal variation that enables subsequent exploration of recurrent patterns of fate 124 

distributions, as well as the analysis of associated molecular features in clonal data. Starting with 125 

a continuous representation of expression state, clone2vec embeds clonal data into a metric low 126 

dimensional space where clones with similar fate profiles are grouped together. Applied to a 127 

variety of clonal tracing datasets, we show that such embeddings provide an informative overview 128 

of clonal variation that is robust to sparse sampling. Across embryonic development, human 129 

cortical differentiation, and hematopoiesis, clone2vec effectively recovers known clonal patterns 130 

while also revealing previously unrecognized substructures. In immuno-oncology datasets 131 

spanning diverse tumor types, it resolves a conserved organization of T lymphocyte clones, 132

Results 139 

> 140

Identification of patterns in sparsely sampled clonal distributions 141 

> 142

The analysis of clonal variation generally aims to identify recurrent structured clonal relationships 143 

between cells, connecting these patterns to cellular phenotypes and associated molecular 144 

features. Assuming that lineage-enabled data generally identifies subsets of cells that originated 145 

from the same progenitor cell, we focus on the most basic relationship: similarity of clonal progeny 146 

across the cellular expression or state manifold (Fig. 1a and Supplementary Fig. 1f-h). At the limit 147 

of dense sampling, progeny can be modeled as distributions and compared using traditional 148 

measures such as Wasserstein distance optimal transport (OT) or maximum mean discrepancy 149 

(MMD) 33 . However, inferring and comparing such distributions becomes difficult when the 150 

sampling is sparse. In most clonal datasets, the distribution of clonal sizes is highly uneven, with 151 

most clones being captured by only a handful of cells, while select few are represented by many 152 

cells (Supplementary Fig. 1e). This combination of sparsity and imbalance hampers stable 153 

estimation of inter-clone similarity, proper identification of which is essential for further data-driven 154 

hypothesis generation and testing (Fig. 1b-d). 155 

> 156

To address this problem, we developed clone2vec – a method inspired by the word2vec algorithm 157 

for capturing semantic relationships between words 34,35 . Clone2vec first approximates the cellular 158 

expression manifold using k-nearest-neighbor graph and uses it to identify which clones co-occur 159 

within local neighborhoods (Fig. 1e). It then learns a low-dimensional embedding of clones that 160 

best preserves these neighborhood relationships, following a Skip-gram-style objective (Fig. 1f-161 

g). The method can be viewed as a generalized PCA factorization of a neighborhood graph – 162 

where exponential family PCA is used to factorize clone-by-clone co-occurrence matrix 36 . Unlike 163 

OT or MMD measures that estimate and compare entire empirical distributions, clone2vec 164 

aggregates discrete clonal co-occurrence events across expression manifold; we also reasoned 165 

that multinomial modelling would yield more robust dimensional reduction under sparse and 166 

heavy-tailed clone size distributions 37 .167 

> 168

To evaluate clone2vec's ability to identify recurrent clonal patterns in sparse sampling regime, we 169 

used a synthetic benchmark (Supplementary Fig. 2-4). We simulated distinct patterns 170 

(Supplementary Fig. 3a-d) of clonal distributions on a simple manifold, and compared the 171 

performance of clone2vec, Skinkhorn divergence, MMD, and cluster-based similarity measures 172 

in recovering sets of clones originating from distinct patterns under increasing levels of sparsity 173 

(Supplementary Fig. 4a). Indeed, we find that clone2vec shows improved pattern recovery 174 

compared to other methods, particularly at high sparsity regimes (Supplementary Fig. 4). 175

The low-dimensional embedding produced by clone2vec is also analytically convenient, as it 191 

supports vector-space algebra. For example, if some clones contribute exclusively to cell type A 192 

and others exclusively to B, a clone contributing equally to A and B would be placed at the 193 

geometric midpoint between the A- and B-associated clones (Supplementary Fig. 2g-l). Overall, 194 

clone2vec embeddings efficiently capture compositional differences between clones in a cluster-195 

free manner, allowing unbiased examination of clonal diversity in the single-cell lineage tracing 196 

data. 197

Analysis of genes associated with various aspects of clonal variation 281 

> 282

A recurring question in the analysis of clonal heterogeneity is how to identify gene expression 283 

programs associated with different aspects of clonal behavior. Current analyses have generally 284 

focused on targeted tests, typically relating gene expression to the likelihood of a particular cell 285 

fate. Such tests can be difficult to interpret when changes involve coordinated shifts among 286 

multiple fates. Clonal embeddings provide an explicit representation of this structure and support 287 

a more unbiased gene-association analysis. 288 

> 289

differentiate into three distinct branches: Tfap2a +, Atoh1 +, and Otp + neurons (Fig. 2e and 292 

Supplementary Fig. 5j). Clones in cluster 0 span progenitors together with the Tfap2a + and Otp +

hematopoiesis from Weinreb et al. 19 (Supplementary Fig. 8). As in the original paper, the analysis 309 

focused on comparison of two monocyte-contributing lineages, only one of which contributed to 310 

neutrophils. Comparing the two clonal clusters which captured this phenotypic variation, 311 

clone2vec re-analysis recapitulated associated expression differences in both progenitor ( Mpo ,312 

Elane , Ctsg ) and differentiated monocyte populations ( Lrg1 , S100a8 ) (Supplementary Fig. 8h,i). 313 

In this relatively simple case, the distinction between these two clonal behaviors could be obtained 314 

via simple gating on the presence or absence of the neutrophil fate, as was done in the original 315 

publication. The advantage of clone2vec embeddings and clonal clusters, however, is that they 316 

can capture more complex scenarios, such as those involving changes in multiple cell fates, or 317 

situations where cell annotation is not obvious or not sufficiently informative about differences in 318 

clonal behaviors. 319 

> 320

To extend the expression associations analysis beyond the cluster-level comparisons, we 321 

implemented gene-association testing directly on clonal embeddings, using supervised PCA 322 

approach (sPCA) in which clone-clone similarities from the embedding define the target kernel, 323 

and gene-level association is assessed via contributions to the resulting supervised 324 

components 41,42 (Supplementary Fig. 8j-k). Application of this strategy to the Haan et al. dataset 325 

showed the first supervised component associated with clonal embedding to be driven by genes 326 

known to be involved in dorso-ventral patterning ( Ntn1 , Spon1 , Ank2 vs. Gli3 , Ptn , Pax3 , Zic1 )327 

(Fig. 2k-l). This was consistent with the spatial transcriptomics data (Fig. 2m-o), demonstrating 328 

that the strategy can identify gene programs linked to broad clonal phenotypic structure. 329 

> 330

Analogous unsupervised strategy can also be applied to test for the association of clonal 331 

phenotypes with gene expression in a particular cell type. Returning to the in vitro hematopoeisis 332 

example from Weinreb et al., we applied this strategy to examine clonal patterns involving 333 

monocytes. We first constructed a new clonal embedding that masked the heterogeneity within 334 

the monocyte population itself, by collapsing all monocytes into a single state (Supplementary 335 

Fig. 8l). Such clonal embedding is sensitive to overall proportion of monocytes, but not sensitive 336 

to their finer state features. We then applied sPCA to identify major expression within monocytes 337 

that are associated with the captured clonal variation. The expected neutrophil axis described 338 

earlier was captured by the second component, with the top genes matching neutrophil-biasing 339 

genes described by Weinreb et al. (Supplementary Fig. 8n-p). Surprisingly, the first component 340 

was linked to the proportion of undifferentiated cells (Supplementary Fig. 8m) – the axis of 341 

variation that could be overseen in direct hypothesis-driven testing. 342

Analysis of clonal variation in large-scale immune datasets 349 

> 350

In lymphocyte populations, receptor sequences can serve as endogenous clonal barcodes. The 351 

genes encoding both T and B cell receptors (TCRs and BCRs) undergo V(D)J recombination 352 

during lymphocyte development generates highly diverse TCR and BCR junctions. Single-cell 353 

assays have been optimized to capture the most variable portion of the receptor, typically 354 

summarized by the complementarity-determining region 3 (CDR3) sequence 43 . For T cells, this 355 

rearranged sequence is generally stable and directly defines a clonal population, while in B cells 356 

antigen-driven somatic hypermutation produces a set of related sequences that together 357 

constitute a clonal subpopulation. 358 

> 359

To illustrate clone2vec for immune-receptor analysis, we first examined clonal structure in 360 

peripheral blood mononuclear cells (PBMCs) from healthy adults. Given recent technological 361 

advances and the relative ease of sampling, PBMC datasets can now reach millions of cells 44,45 .362 

To support analysis at this scale, we draw on the recent theoretical results showing that the Skip-363 

Gram objective utilized by clone2vec can be effectively approximated by applying Poisson GLM-364 

PCA to the matrix of clone-neighbor co-occurrences 36,37,46 (see Methods). This formulation relies 365 

on the assumption that neighbor counts are generated via a log-linear interaction of latent 366 

embeddings, allowing us to recover comparable clonal embeddings with significantly improved 367 

computational scalability (Supplementary Fig. 2-4). 368 

> 369

Applying clone2vec with Poisson loss to a PBMCs dataset from Sureshchandra et al. 45 covering 370 

almost 2.3 million cells from 10 healthy donors (Supplementary Fig. 9a), of which 208,683 cells 371 

are part of 23,362 clones of size three and more (Supplementary Fig. 9b), we found four main 372 

clonal clusters corresponding to two general lineages of CD8 + T cells (MAIT and memory) as well 373 

as two main lineages of CD4 + T cells (memory and regulatory) (Fig. 3a-b and Supplementary Fig. 374 

9c-d). Unlike developmental or differentiation settings, where clones often span multiple cell 375 

types, from progenitor to effector types, PBMC clonal clusters are largely restricted to individual 376 

cell types. This is expected because the receptor rearrangement that “barcodes” the cell typically 377 

occurs alongside lineage commitment, and subsequent inter-lineage transitions are rare 47 .378 

Nonetheless, as we show in the next two sections, receptor-defined clonal structure can uncover 379 

functionally critical variations within T lymphocytes, distinguishing the developmental origins of T 380 

cell populations, or linking transcriptional states to their history of antigen-driven activation and 381 

selection. 382

Clonal embeddings help to separate thymic and peripherally induced Tregs 405 

> 406

Regulatory T cells (Tregs), essential for providing immunotolerance to self, are increasingly 407 

understood to arise through distinct developmental routes: thymus-derived Tregs (tTregs), and 408 

peripherally induced Tregs (pTregs) generated from conventional CD4 T cells under tolerogenic 409 

conditions 48–50 . Furthermore, the pTreg compartment is intimately linked to the plasticity of the 410 

Th17 lineage; during the resolution of inflammation, Th17 effectors can undergo context-411 

dependent reprogramming and acquire a regulatory transcriptional program and suppressive 412 

function, including FOXP3 -associated states 51–53 . Distinguishing thymic versus peripheral origin 413 

can be challenging, particularly in human studies, as commonly used markers can reflect 414 

activation or tissue context rather than lineage history, complicating efforts to map “origin” onto 415 

functional state and clonal architecture 54,55 .416 

> 417

As Tregs-containing clones from PBMC appeared to be quite homogeneous, we decided to 418 

examine clonal heterogeneity of Treg-associated lineages within the tonsillar part of the same 419 

dataset (Supplementary Fig. 9e), consisting of 105,978 clones of size three and more (covering 420 

702,839 cells). Indeed, tonsillar T cell clones showed much higher level of clonal heterogeneity, 421 

mostly related to the CD4 T cells lineages (Fig. 3c and Supplementary Fig. 9f-i). Focused analysis 422 

of Treg-containing clones revealed three well-separated clonal subtypes with distinct 423 

transcriptional signatures (Fig. 3d-e). Treg clonal cluster 2 was notable for containing Th17 cells 424 

alongside a distinct Treg state with high RORC and low IKZF2 , which is consistent with pTreg 425 

markers established in murine studies 54,56–59 (Fig. 3f). Additional evidence supporting distinct 426 

origin of the putative pTreg cluster comes from the fate distribution of these clones in peripheral 427 

blood. The experimental design of the original study included matching PBMC and tonsil samples 428 

from the same donors. In blood, the sister cells of clones from the putative tonsillar pTreg cluster 429 

2, were significantly more likely to be found among memory T cells, rather than Ttregs (Fig. 3g,h). 430 

In contrast, the sister cells of the other two tonsillar clonal clusters were mostly found among 431 

PBMC Tregs. 432 

> 433

As Tregs are abundant in colonic mucosa, we next examined single-cell data on colorectal 434 

cancer 60 . Clone2vec embeddings largely consist of clusters with the major annotated T cell types 435 

(Supplementary Fig. 10a,b), but within the Treg compartment we consistently observed two well-436 

separated clonal clusters that remained stable upon focused re-embedding (Fig. 3i and 437 

Supplementary Fig. 10c-f). Treg clonal cluster 0 was notable for containing Th17 cells (Fig. 3j) 438 

alongside a distinct Treg state with high RORC and low IKZF2 (Supplementary Fig. 10g), 439 

consistent with our previous observations. Comparison of clonotype sharing with matched PBMCs 440 

from the same patients suggested divergent lineage connectivity: while IKZF2 + tTreg-like cells 441 

displayed highly Treg-restricted clone sharing, RORC + pTreg-like cells shared clonotypes more 442 

broadly with circulating CD4 T cell states, including ANXA1 + Tcm-like cells (Fig. 3k). 443 

> 444

We next asked whether this clonal partitioning of Tregs is conserved in other inflammatory 445 

contexts by analyzing an independent colon dataset from checkpoint inhibitor-associated colitis 446 

by Luoma et al 61 . Here, clone2vec analysis again separated Tregs into two clonal clusters with a 447 

concordant marker program (Fig. 3l-n and Supplementary Fig. 10m-n): an IKZF3 + pTreg-like 448

Archetypal lineages of CD8 + T cells in tumor microenvironment 464 

> 465

Next, we aimed to illustrate how clonal analysis of immune receptor sequences can resolve clonal 466 

structures within an individual cell type that may reflect prior antigen exposure, expansion history, 467 

or microenvironmental cues. This is particularly important for tumor microenvironment CD8 + T468 

cells, which can occupy similar transcriptional states despite very different histories: recent 469 

trafficking vs. long-term residency, bystander infiltration vs. tumor-antigen engagement, or acute 470 

vs. chronic stimulation 62 . As checkpoint blockade therapies aim to amplify effective anti-tumor 471 

CD8 + T cell immunity, clarifying which clones are likely tumor-reactive and how they differentiate 472 

is of immediate therapeutic relevance 63 . We therefore analyzed data on non-small cell lung cancer 473 

(NSCLC) by Caushi et al. 31 , which analyzed matched tumor, adjacent normal and peripheral blood 474 

samples with both TCR sequencing and orthogonal antigen-specificity assays for representative 475 

clonotypes, providing an opportunity to connect clonal structure to antigen experience 476 

(Supplementary Fig. 11a-b). 477 

> 478

Restricting to conventional CD8 + T cells (excluding innate-like CD8 + populations such as MAIT 479 

cells), we constructed clone2vec embeddings for TCR-defined clones across the cohort from 480 

Caushi et al. 31 (Supplementary Fig. 11c,d). Rather than forming a small number of sharply 481 

separated clone “types,” the clone embedding space showed a graded spectrum of behaviors 482 

(Supplementary Fig. 11e), consistent with tumor-infiltrating CD8 + T spanning multiple activation, 483 

residency, and dysfunction programs despite sharing a common cell identity 31,62 . We therefore 484 

summarized this structure using archetypal analysis (Fig. 4a-d), yielding four dominant clone-level 485 

programs that we refer to by their most discriminative markers: CXCL13 High , IL7R High , GZMK High ,486 

and S1PR5 High (Fig. 4e). 487 

> 488

Interpreting these programs using the study’s orthogonal antigen-specificity measurements and 489 

cross-compartment sampling, we found that each archetype likely corresponds to a distinct history 490 

of antigen exposure. CXCL13 High clones expressed a canonical dysfunction signature (including 491 

ENTPD1 /CD39) and accounted for almost all of the TCRs with an experimentally validated affinity 492

Alignment of clonal embeddings shows conserved CD8 + T cell lineages across cancer types 523 

> 524

In addition to analysis of clonal variation in individual datasets, it is often important to identify 525 

patterns of clonal variation recur across biological contexts. Here we asked if the patterns of clonal 526 

variation observed within the CD8 + T cells identified in NSCLC generalize to other tumor types. 527 

To do so we first used clone2vec to analyze datasets from NSCLC by Liu et al. 66 , colorectal cancer 528 

by Chen et al. 60 , head and neck squamous cell carcinoma (HNSCC) by McCord et al .67 , as well 529 

as glioblastoma and a panel of brain metastases of different origins by Wang et al .68 Across these 530 

diverse datasets, the clonal embedding space frequently exhibited a similar organization, with 531 

clonal clusters marked by overlapping gene expression signatures (Fig. 5a-c and Supplementary 532 

Fig. 12), suggesting that major CD8 + clonal programs are not unique to NSCLC. 533 

> 534

In principle, joint analysis of multiple datasets could proceed by integrating all single cells across 535 

studies and recomputing a unified clonal embedding. In practice, integrating T cells across diverse 536 

tumor types is challenging and risks overcorrecting lineage-specific differences. We therefore 537 

introduced a method to align clonal spaces directly, taking advantage of their algebraic structure 538 

(see Methods). Briefly, the approach used mutual nearest neighbors 69 to match similar clones 539 

across datasets based on their average gene expression profile (Fig. 5d), and then found the best 540 

linear transformation to optimize their alignment in the joint latent space (Fig. 5e-f). Such a 541 

procedure guarantees that the algebraic structure of the individual clonal spaces will hold even 542 

after an integration. 543 

> 544

The resulting integration of six different clonal embeddings has recapitulated the structure of the 545 

embedding initially observed for the NSCLC cohort Caushi et al. (Fig. 5g), described by four major 546 

archetypes associated with similar transcriptional signatures (Fig. 5h-j). Similarly, the patterns of 547 

clonal variation observed in analysis of other individual datasets were also well captured by the 548 

archetypes identified in the joint analysis (Fig. 5j and Supplementary Fig. 13). The robust 549 

recurrence of clonal variation patterns across diverse cancer types and tissues indicates that 550 

these archetypes capture general behavior of CD8 + T cells rather than specifics of tumor 551 

microenvironment in different tissues. 552 

> 553

When aligning diverse datasets, it is also possible to relax the analytical constraints on clonal 554 

space, which can yield more structured clonal embeddings. Some of the existing methods allow 555 

decoupling the space used to establish similarity between datasets and the space where the 556 

integration is actually performed. For example, applying Seurat v5 70 to integrate multi-cancer 557 

CD8+ T cell lineage data, shows an embedding that is consistent with the archetypes described 558 

earlier, but has a more pronounced cluster structure (Supplementary Fig. 14). An alignment 559 

preserving linear constraints, however, is more appropriate for the archetypal analysis. 560

Discussion 604 

605 

As lineage-coupled single-cell profiling becomes more common, effective analysis will likely 606 

involve multiple computational strategies. Clone2vec implements one such strategy, centered on 607 

constructing a continuous clonal embedding to enable an unbiased, top-level exploration of 608 

prevalent clonal variation. The approach is focused on clones as indivisible analysis units (Supp. 609 

Fig. 14). By projecting clones into an informative low-dimensional space, clone2vec allows 610 

investigators to identify distinct clonal behaviors and follow them up by characterizing specific fate 611 

distributions and associated molecular programs. These embeddings are sensitive not only to 612 

which cell types are present within a clone, but also to their relative proportions and detailed 613 

states, allowing clone2vec to capture finer aspects of clonal variation than approaches based 614

It is made The copyright holder for this preprint (which this version posted May 5, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.30.720820 doi: bioRxiv preprint

balance, partial biases, and graded differences between clones that would otherwise be 616 

overlooked. The resulting embedding provides a high-resolution description of clonal identity that 617 

can be linked directly to specific gene expression features, positional and anatomical coordinates, 618 

or developmental timing. In this way, clonal variation can be analyzed not as a set of coarse, 619 

predefined classes, but as a continuous and biologically interpretable landscape spanning lineage 620 

output, molecular state, spatial context, and temporal progression. 621 

> 622

Fundamentally, comparing two clones amounts to comparing their distributions over the cellular 623 

expression manifold. Although many methods exist for comparing distributions based on samples, 624 

lineage-traced scRNA-seq data pose distinct challenges, stemming from small clone sizes, 625 

uneven clone-size distributions, and stochastic noise. Under these sparse regimes, the skip-gram 626 

approach implemented by clone2vec is significantly more robust than more common metrics like 627 

optimal transport or maximum mean discrepancy, allowing for a more stable recovery of the 628 

underlying biological structure. 629 

> 630

An important illustration of the robustness of the clone2vec approach is the pan-cancer recurrence 631 

of the same four CD8 + T cell clonal subpopulations across a diverse range of solid tumors (Supp. 632 

Fig. 12-13); in the absence of ground-truth labels for clonal architecture, this cross-cohort 633 

reproducibility across independently generated datasets also serves as a real-data benchmark 634 

for the method. It suggests a highly conserved, multi-compartment model of TME infiltration and 635 

clonal fate determination. We interpret the S1PR5 High archetype as the circulating precursor or 636 

recently infiltrated pool, consistent with its enrichment in blood and the experimental evidence 637 

that S1PR5 limits durable tissue residency 71 . Following tissue infiltration, clonal trajectories 638 

appear to bifurcate based on local TCR engagement. Clones recognizing tumor neoantigens are 639 

driven into the CXCL13 High archetype, a state characterized by chronic activation and canonical 640 

exhaustion markers ( e.g. ENTPD1 , CD39 , CTLA4 ). Conversely, infiltrating clones lacking tumor-641 

antigen affinity assume distinct bystander roles likely maintained by local inflammatory cytokines 642 

rather than direct tumor-antigen stimulation. The IL7R High archetype, enriched in adjacent normal 643 

tissue and associated with influenza-reactive clonotypes, is most consistent with a tissue-644 

associated memory-like bystander population 62,72 . The GZMK High archetype may instead reflect a 645 

more inflammatory intratumoral bystander state, mobilized by cytokine-rich conditions within TME. 646 

While the enrichment for CMV- and EBV-reactive clonotypes suggests a potential contribution 647 

from latent antiviral memory, it may represent a broader GZMK + CD8 + tumor-infiltrating program 648 

previously observed across multiple cancers 62,68,73 . This interpretation is also consistent with the 649 

observed scarcity of transitions between archetypes during anti-PD-1 therapy, as one would not 650 

expect checkpoint blockade to alter archetype membership if it is fundamentally determined by 651 

inherent TCR affinities. 652 

> 653

The clone2vec approach should generalize well to other modalities and tracing techniques. The 654 

presented examples are mostly focused on the available scRNA-seq datasets, however, the 655 

graph-based approach employed for assessing molecular similarity between cells is directly 656 

applicable to analysis of lineage-tracing coupled to other modalities ( e.g. ATAC-seq, etc. ). In 657 

terms of tracing techniques, the examples are focused on applications to static barcodes, 658

Expanding the approach to dynamic barcoding techniques, both prospective 8,74 , as well as 660

> 662

The biological phenomena captured by any clonal embedding depend on the experimental 663 

design, particularly on the timing of barcode induction. In developmental studies, for example, 664 

barcodes can be introduced early, before substantial commitment has occurred. The clonal 665 

embeddings will then capture variation in progenitor competence and differentiation bias 8,38 . In 666 

contrast, TCRs are formed together with cell type commitment, and the clonal variation will reflect 667 

plasticity and clonal restrictions between different states of a single, committed cell type. In this 668 

setting, clone2vec is advantageous because it does not depend on predefined cell annotations, 669 

but instead quantifies clonal similarity directly from cell distributions in continuous expression 670 

space, allowing unbiased exploration of state structure and enabling potential discovery of cell 671 

subpopulations distinguished by their clonal relationships. 672 

> 673

A key strategy to follow up on the observed clonal variation patterns is to link them to the 674 

underlying molecular signatures. We have described several such common test scenarios. For 675 

example, in a differentiating context, one would commonly test for genes in progenitor populations 676 

associated with downstream lineage biases (Fig 2g,h). Conversely, testing for expression 677 

associations in a distinct mature cell population can be used to reveal elaborate coordination of 678 

cell behaviors in a tissue, such as the illustrated difference in the state of monocyte cells in clones 679 

that do or do not contribute to neutrophils (Supplementary Fig. 8a-h) 19 . Finally, clone2vec also 680 

implements global association tests, to identify gene expression patterns in all or select cell 681 

subpopulations that explain as much of the overall observed clonal variation as possible (Fig. 2m-682 

o). When the goal is to link early progenitor state to later fate, dedicated frameworks such as 683 

CoSpar 28 may be preferable because they explicitly exploit manifold continuity and can borrow 684 

information from unlabeled cells, thereby increasing the effective sample size for fate-bias 685 

inference. Clone2vec should therefore be viewed as complementary to these approaches: it is 686 

strongest as an annotation-independent, exploratory representation of clonal variation that 687 

supports hypothesis generation, cross-context comparison and subsequent targeted testing.As 688 

lineage-enabled assays continue to scale, we hope clone2vec will be instrumental in extracting 689 

biological insights from such data. 690

Methods 735 

736 

clone2vec algorithm 737 

738 

At its core, clone2vec can be viewed as an algorithm for the multinomial matrix factorization of a clone-by-clone nearest-739 

neighbor co-occurrence matrix. In more detail, the algorithm consists of the following steps (for more formal description 740 

see Supplementary Note). 741 

742 

1. Construction of an appropriate gene expression embedding 743 

The algorithm relies on two inputs: (a) a gene expression embedding and (b) clonal labels. The gene expression 744 

embedding is a low-dimensional representation of cells (typically 15-30 dimensions) that captures the biologically 745 

relevant variance in the data. In standard pipelines, it can be PCA computed from gene expression; for multi-batch 746 

datasets, it can be a batch-corrected embedding such as Harmony 77 , an MNN-corrected PCA space (as in Seurat 70 ), 747 

or a VAE latent representation (as in scVI 78 ). Proper batch correction in this space is essential. Otherwise, cells from 748 

> .CC-BY 4.0 International license available under a was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. It is made The copyright holder for this preprint (which this version posted May 5, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.30.720820 doi: bioRxiv preprint

21 

the same batch will group together in the final clonal embedding because of residual batch effects rather than shared 749 

biology – the batch signal propagates through the kNN graph into the clone-level representation. The clonal labels form 750 

a single vector assigning a clonal identity to each cell. For evolving-barcode lineage tracing, an appropriate level of the 751 

lineage tree must be chosen to collapse cells into a single flat set of clones. 752 

753 

2. kNN graph construction and clonal neighborhood aggregation 754 

Using the batch-corrected gene expression embedding, we build a k-nearest-neighbor (kNN) graph on clonally labeled 755 

cells (for example, with k = 15). From this graph, for each cell we count how many of its k neighbors belong to each 756 

clone, producing a vector of length C, where C is the number of unique clones in the dataset. We then aggregate these 757 

per-cell vectors to the clone level by summing them across all cells of the same clone. The result is a C × C matrix 758 

whose entry (i, j) is the total number of neighbors from clone j observed in the gene expression neighborhoods of all 759 

cells from clone i.760 

761 

3. Skip-Gram 762 

This step can be described in two mathematically equivalent ways 36 – via a Skip-Gram neural network or via multinomial 763 

GLM-PCA – and for now we focus on the Skip-Gram formulation. Skip-Gram is a simple neural network with a single 764 

hidden layer of size z (the number of latent dimensions in clone2vec; typically z is 10) and input and output layers of 765 

size C. The activations in the hidden and output layers are linear. With no further modifications and an MSE loss, this 766 

architecture is equivalent to PCA 79 . Non-linearity is introduced by applying a softmax to the output layer, which 767 

transforms the real-valued output scores into a probability distribution over clones. These predicted probabilities are 768 

compared with the empirical probabilities of observing one clone in the gene expression neighborhood of another, and 769 

the model is trained by minimizing the resulting negative log-likelihood. Training is performed in shuffled mini-batches, 770 

using individual pairs of clones as elementary training examples; this is what makes the present implementation closer 771 

to a Skip-Gram network than to GLM-PCA, where the full matrix is typically fit at once. After convergence, the input-to-772 

hidden weight matrix is used as the latent representation of clones. 773 

774 

Poisson GLM-PCA as an alternative to Skip-Gram 775 

776 

As noted above, Skip-Gram can be equivalently formulated as multinomial GLM-PCA (Generalized Linear Model PCA) 777 

– the same model is also referred to as multinomial EPCA (Exponential-family PCA) in the earlier literature 37 . In the 778 

multinomial formulation, efficient parallel optimization is limited because the softmax normalization couples all output 779 

units. In natural language processing, negative sampling – a procedure in which the full softmax is replaced by a binary 780 

classification against a small set of sampled negative examples – is routinely used for large corpora; its computational 781 

advantages are most pronounced when the output vocabulary is very large and the embedding dimension is also large 782 

(e.g., 200-300), and are minimal or even detrimental at the small embedding dimensions used in clone2vec ( z = 10 ). 783 

784 

If the multinomial likelihood is replaced by a Poisson likelihood, each output unit becomes conditionally independent of 785 

the others, which enables efficient parallel optimization via Alternative Poisson regression, as it’s shown in fastglmpca 46 

786 

(now implemented in fastglmpca-py). With column-wise and row-wise normalization, the resulting embeddings become 787 

comparable to those of the multinomial model, making the Poisson variant a fast alternative to standard clone2vec for 788 

datasets whose size would otherwise be prohibitive. In ordinary cases, however, we recommend the vanilla multinomial 789 

clone2vec, as it more faithfully captures the compositional structure of the neighborhood counts and offers stronger 790 

theoretical guarantees on the resulting solution. 791

Clustering and archetypal analysis in clone2vec space 793 

794 

The output of clone2vec – a latent representation of each clone – can serve as input to the standard downstream 795 

analyses developed for single-cell RNA-seq data. Throughout the paper, we use UMAP 80 visualizations of the 796 

clone2vec space to inspect its local structure and, in some cases, trajectory inference methods to characterize 797 

continuous axes of variation in clonal composition. For discrete grouping, we recommend community-detection 798 

algorithms such as Leiden 81 .799 

800 

When the boundaries between clonal clusters are not well defined – a situation that arises frequently in practice, due 801 

to the presence of small clones, genuinely fuzzy boundaries between cell types, and the inherently continuous nature 802 

> .CC-BY 4.0 International license available under a was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. of compositional variation across clones – archetypal analysis provides a complementary view of the heterogeneity in 803 

clonal behavior. In brief, archetypal analysis fits a convex polytope with A vertices, called archetypes, to the data under 804 

two requirements: each data point is approximated as a convex combination of the archetypes, and each archetype is 805 

itself a convex combination of data points 82 . The archetypes therefore lie near the extremes of the data distribution and 806 

act as interpretable reference points, with every clone expressed as a mixture of them. Because the clone2vec space 807 

preserves the compositional semantics of clones in its geometric structure, the resulting archetypes tend to recover 808 

stereotypical patterns of clonal behavior that jointly account for the bulk of the observed variation. After this analysis, 809 

each clone is represented by its coordinates in archetype space – by the weights of its convex combination over the A 810 

archetypes. These coordinates can be reduced to a hard, cluster-like assignment by labelling each clone with the 811 

archetype that carries the largest weight. 812

Cluster-level gene expression associations 814 

815 

Once the clonal variation of interest has been characterized – by building a trajectory and assigning a pseudotime value 816 

to each clone, by clustering, or by archetypal analysis – the resulting groupings or orderings can be explored with 817 

classical differential expression techniques. We recommend treating clones, rather than individual cells, as the units of 818 

replication in these tests: for each clone, gene expression is averaged across the cells of the cell type of interest, and 819 

differential expression is then performed on the resulting clone-level pseudobulk profiles. 820 

821 

Cluster-free gene expression associations analysis 822 

823 

To exploit the continuous and multidimensional nature of clone2vec embeddings, rather than collapsing them into 824 

discrete labels, we provide a framework for cluster-free analysis of the association between gene expression and 825 

position in clone2vec space, based on supervised PCA 42 . The idea is to use clone2vec coordinates to define which 826 

clones are close to each other, and then to look for patterns of gene expression that vary smoothly across that 827 

neighborhood structure – i.e. patterns in which clones that are close in clone2vec space also tend to have similar 828 

expression. 829 

830 

Concretely, we build a k-nearest-neighbor graph on the clones in clone2vec space, and then identify the directions in 831 

gene expression space along which neighboring clones are most similar. These directions form a small set of graph-832 

aware principal components, each corresponding to a coordinated program of genes whose expression changes 833 

gradually as one moves across the clone2vec manifold. Each component comes with an associated measure of how 834 

much of the smooth, neighbor-structured variation it captures, which allows the components to be ranked by relevance 835 

and truncated as in ordinary PCA. Top-ranked components correspond to the broadest patterns of variation – smooth 836 

gradients that span large regions of the clone2vec space and reflect gene programs differentiating major clonal 837 

behaviors – while lower-ranked components capture progressively finer-scale, more localized patterns, down to subtle 838 

expression differences among nearby clones. 839 

840 

As a univariate complement to this joint decomposition, we compute Moran's I 83 for each gene – a standard measure 841 

of the extent to which a signal is organized by an underlying graph structure – and test, for every gene, whether its 842 

expression is more spatially structured in clone2vec space than would be expected by chance. The resulting per-gene 843 

p-values are adjusted for multiple testing with the Benjamini-Hochberg procedure. Together, the graph-aware principal 844 

components describe the dominant coordinated patterns of expression variation across the clone2vec manifold, while 845 

Moran's I flags individual genes whose expression is significantly shaped by clone2vec geometry. 846

Horizontal alignment of clonal embeddings 848 

849 

Jointly analyzing several clone2vec embeddings from different cohorts is an additional computational challenge that 850 

arose during method development. Standard integration of multiple single-cell datasets at the cell level can erase 851 

biologically meaningful variation and complicate interpretation of the resulting embedding. To avoid this, we developed 852 

an integration procedure that operates directly on clonal embeddings. 853 

854 

The central difficulty is that clone2vec is trained independently for each cohort, and its latent space is identifiable only 855 

up to an arbitrary linear transformation: the axes carry no intrinsic meaning in terms of cell-type composition, and 856 

> .CC-BY 4.0 International license available under a was not certified by peer review) is the author/funder, who has granted bioRxiv a license to display the preprint in perpetuity. It is made The copyright holder for this preprint (which this version posted May 5, 2026. ;https://doi.org/10.64898/2026.04.30.720820 doi: bioRxiv preprint

23 

coordinates are not directly comparable across cohorts. An external reference is therefore required to match clones 857 

between datasets. We use the average gene expression per clone together with a mutual nearest neighbor (MNN) 858 

strategy 69 , through the following multi-step procedure: 859 

860 

1. Per-clone average gene expression is computed by averaging the log-normalized expression of all genes 861 

across the cells assigned to each clone. 862 

2. Clones from all cohorts are concatenated into a single dataset in which each clone is treated as a pseudo-863 

cell. 864 

3. Highly variable genes are identified in a batch-aware manner, with cohort as the batch variable. 865 

4. Expression is scaled per cohort prior to PCA. 866 

5. PCA is computed on the scaled expression matrix. 867 

6. The resulting PCA space is integrated across cohorts with Harmony. 868 

7. Mutual nearest neighbors are identified between every pair of cohorts in the Harmony-corrected space. 869 

870 

The matched clone pairs – which can be referred to as anchors, following Seurat's terminology 84 – are then used to 871 

bring the clone2vec coordinates of different cohorts into a common frame. Alignment is performed hierarchically: 872 

cohorts, or groups of already-aligned cohorts, are merged in order of decreasing number of shared anchors, so that 873 

the most strongly connected cohorts are integrated first. At each merge step, the smaller cohort (or group) is mapped 874 

onto the larger one by a weighted affine transformation – a linear map followed by a translation – whose parameters 875 

are obtained by weighted least squares on the anchor pairs, using the weights defined above. The final result is a single 876 

aligned clone2vec embedding in which clones from all cohorts share a common coordinate system. 877

Details of benchmarking 879 

880 

To benchmark the ability of clone2vec to capture continuous changes in clonal composition, we generated a 2D “gene 881 

expression” space with two well-separated clusters (A and B). After that, we generated clones using cells from this 882 

gene expression space so each clone consisted of 10 cells and had exactly n (n = 0, 1, …, 10 ) cells from cluster A and 883 

10 − n cells from cluster B; for each n the procedure was repeated 500 times, resulting in 5500 clones in total. Clonal 884 

embedding was built with only two latent dimensions, and the first principal component of the clone2vec space was 885 

used to find a correlation between the position of the clone on a clonal embedding and the proportion of cell type A in 886 

the clone. In the case of Poisson GLM-PCA benchmarking, the first GLM-PC was used to estimate the correlation. 887 

888 

To evaluate the ability to capture visibly distinct clonal behaviors and support semantic geometry, we generated a 2D 889 

“gene expression” space with three clusters (A, B, and C); after that, we generated clones (each of size 15) consisting 890 

of three “portions” (of 5 cells each): each “portion” can be from cluster A, B, or C. Depending on which clusters were 891 

involved in the clone construction, we labeled these clones as “AAA” (for clones with 15 cells from cluster A), “ABB” 892 

(for clones with 5 cells from A and 10 cells from B), “ABC” (for clones with 5 cells from each cluster), and so on. Clonal 893 

embedding with a latent space of size 2 was built based on 500 clones of each type (totaling 5000 clones), and the 894 

resulting geometry was evaluated visually. 895 

896 

For more complex clonal distributions (“Rings and Crosses” benchmark), we constructed a 2D “gene expression” space 897 

containing four concentric rings and six crosses rotated at different angles (0°, 15°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 75°). Clones of 898 

size 30 were generated by sampling cells from these specific geometric structures (one clone is sampled from one 899 

specific structure). For each of the ten structures (four rings and six crosses), 300 clones were simulated, resulting in 900 

3000 clones in total. After that, the dataset was subsampled into subsets consisting of 1%, 5%, 10%, 25%, and 50% of 901 

the original data. Using these subsets, clonal embeddings for clones with size ≥ 2 were built. Using the same set of 902 

clones and the location of cells from them in the original 2D “expression space”, we calculated a variety of different 903 

metrics (such as MMD, Sinkhorn divergence, Energy distance, and cluster-based composition). Шт situations where 904 

the method returns distances (MMD, Sinkhorn, Energy) instead of vector representations, we used these distances 905 

directly for kNN graph construction and clustering. The performance was evaluated by performing Leiden clustering on 906 

the clonal embedding and comparing the results to the ground truth labels using the Adjusted Rand Index (ARI), 907 

searching for the best-matching clustering resolution starting from 0.1 and ending with 2 with a step of 0.1. 908

Unless stated otherwise, processing started from raw counts, which were library-size normalized (such that the total 912 

count per cell summed to 10,000 UMIs), after which 3,000 highly variable genes were selected in a batch-aware 913 

manner. These genes were then scaled and used for PCA (typically reduced to 20 principal components). When 914 

multiple batches were present, Harmony77 was used for batch correction. The Harmony-corrected space was used to 915 

build a kNN graph between clonally labelled cells (from clones of size ≥ 3, k = 15) and to calculate the nearest 916 

neighborhood for each clone. This clonal neighborhood matrix was then fed into a Skip-Gram clone2vec model with 10 917 

latent dimensions. The resulting clone2vec space was used to build a clone-to-clone similarity kNN graph (k = 15) for 918 

subsequent clustering and dimensionality reduction with UMAP. For differential expression analysis, each clone was 919 

represented by the average expression across all cells of the cell type of interest, and a Welch’s t-test with Benjamini-920 

Hochberg multiple testing correction was applied to identify genes whose expression differed significantly between 921 

groups of clones. When a subset of clones was selected (e.g., CD8 T cells from all T cells), this was typically done after 922 

the main embedding, which contained all cells of interest, had been built. Assuming that 10 clone2vec dimensions are 923 

sufficient to capture even fine differences between clones, we used only the clones of interest to build a kNN graph on 924 

this subset for further clustering and dimensionality reduction. 925 926 

T cell clonotypes were identified from AIRR/CellRanger VDJ-like files, using a full match of both chains' CDR3 927 

nucleotide sequences as the criterion for grouping cells into clones. Clones containing more than one TRB sequence 928 

were excluded; in the case of multiple TRA sequences per cell, all TRA sequences were required to match within the 929 

clone. All procedures were performed using the scirpy 85 Python package. Antigen lookup in VDJdb 65 was also 930 

performed with scirpy, using the amino acid sequences of the CDR3 regions of both alpha and beta chains. Archetypal 931 

analysis was performed using the ParTIpy 86 Python package, which implements the PCHA 87 algorithm. Four archetypes 932 

were selected based on explained variance, information criteria, and bootstrap stability. In cases where datasets 933 

consisted of multiple biopsies per patient (e.g., tumor / LN / metastasis), only T cells from the TME were used to build 934 

the clonal embedding. 935

Haan et al. 38 Data were obtained from ArrayExpress (E-MTAB-14817). Neurons were subselected from the whole 937 

dataset based on the expression of Tubb3 and Sox1 . Clone-type annotation was performed based on the average 938 

expression across all cells of each clone. In the cluster-free analysis, each clone was represented by multiple data 939 

points corresponding to the number of distinct cell types present in that clone (e.g., a clone containing 3 cell types was 940 

represented by 3 data points sharing the same clone2vec position). In addition, the mean expression per cell type, 941 

computed across all clones, was subtracted from the cell-type-specific expression vector of each individual clone. This 942 

step, together with the previous one, was performed to remove the influence of cell type markers on the SPCA 943 

procedure. The final SPCA was then built using clone2vec-based similarity between the resulting data points (including 944 

multiple replicates of the same clone originating from different cell types) and the scaled expression of each cell-type-945 

restricted clone. Trajectory inference on the clone2vec embedding was performed with the ElPiGraph 88 algorithm 946 

implemented in scFates 89 , using 15 nodes for principal curve fitting based on all 10 dimensions of the clone2vec latent 947 

representation. 948
