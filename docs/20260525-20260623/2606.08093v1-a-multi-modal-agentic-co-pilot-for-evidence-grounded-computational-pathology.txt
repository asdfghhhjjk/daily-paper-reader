## ABSTRACT 

Pathology is the cornerstone of modern medicine, where accurate decision-making relies heavily on evidence-based practices. While artificial intelligence (AI) has the potential to transform clinical workflows, the intersection of AI and evidence-based medicine remains under-explored, with primitive attempts restricted to text-only general medicine. Consequently, evidence-based AI for pathology represents an uncharted domain, lacking systems capable of grounding multimodal information within a rigorous evidence hierarchy. Moreover, existing conventional pathology AI approaches suffer from simplistic knowledge modeling that fails to capture complex clinical relationships, and their real-world value remains unproven due to a lack of rigorous validation. To overcome these limitations, we present PathPocket, a multimodal AI agentic co-pilot designed specifically for evidence grounded pathology. We construct the most comprehensive pathology evidence corpus to date, encompassing approximately 110,472 public and authorized documents structured across a rigorous hierarchy of evidence from clinical guideline to expert opinion. From this meticulously graded foundation, we build a large-scale multimodal pathology hypergraph containing over 4.55 million entities and 7.10 million relations. Serving as a robust knowledge engine, this hypergraph provides traceable evidence for a collaborative multi-agent reasoning framework integrating input understanding, evidence retrieval, filtering, and diagnosis generation. This enables PathPocket to seamlessly resolve a wide spectrum of clinical tasks, ranging from text-only queries to complex multimodal diagnostics involving region-of-interest (ROI) and gigapixel whole-slide images (WSIs). We rigorously evaluate the system on a multidimensional benchmark of over 200,000 real-world cases, where it significantly outperforms existing state-of-the-arts. Crucially, extensive user studies demonstrate that PathPocket substantially improves the diagnostic accuracy and confidence of pathologists. By directly grounding pathology interpretations in verifiable literature, PathPocket offers a practical and scalable solution for the future of evidence grounded computational pathology. 

## 1 Introduction 

Evidence-based medicine 1–3 integrates the best research evidence with clinical expertise and patient values to make informed healthcare decisions. Pathology 4–11 , as the cornerstone of modern medicine, is inherently evidence-driven. Every morphological observation, immunohistochemical interpretation, and molecular finding must be contextualized within established medical knowledge. However, current artificial intelligence (AI) models for pathology 12–22 , despite strong diagnostic performance, are rarely grounded in explicit clinical evidence. While existing clinical AI tools, such as OpenEvidence 25 and the Baichuan-M series 26 , successfully integrate medical evidence, they remain largely confined to text-only general medicine. Crucially, they lack the capacity to process multimodal data, particularly the morphological images that serve as the fundamental diagnostic language in pathology. More recently, evidence-based pathology AI has attracted increasing attention, with several initial frameworks emerging to tackle domain-specific evidence retrieval. YpathRAG 27 , for instance, relies on a corpus of 1,000 papers and uses flat chunk embedding for retrieval, while Patho-AgenticRAG 28 builds upon page-level embeddings from 600 textbooks. Both approaches are unable to capture the structured multi - entity relationships among diseases, biomarkers, morphology, and molecular findings that underpin pathological reasoning, e.g., how a specific disease is linked to a panel of immunohistochemical markers. Furthermore, these systems have been validated on small, often single-modality datasets, leaving their real - world utility uncertain. Here we present PathPocket, a multimodal agentic co-pilot for evidence grounded reasoning in computational pathology (Figure 1 ). First, we curated the most comprehensive pathology evidence corpus assembled to date, containing over 110,000 documents from guidelines, systematic reviews, case reports, and other sources, each tagged with an explicit level of evidence following an eight-tier adaptation of the GRADE 2 hierarchy. From this corpus, we construct a large-scale multimodal hypergraph with 4.55 million entities and 7.10 million relations ( Figure 1A ). Unlike flat databases, hyperedges can connect multiple nodes simultaneously. Second, to support both the large-scale construction of this hypergraph and dynamic data processing, we establish a robust multimodal infrastructure ( Figure 1B ), encompassing document parsing, multimodal embeddings, patch selection, and reranking. Third, this infrastructure and hypergraph drive a collaborative multi-agent reasoning framework (Figure 1C ) in which specialized agents for case understanding, evidence retrieval, filtering, and diagnosis generation, with every final answer carrying direct, verifiable citations. Finally, we demonstrate that PathPocket substantially outperforms existing systems ( Figure 1D ) on a benchmark with over 20,000 text-only, ROI-level, and WSI-level cases. 

## 2 Results 

In this study, we develop and comprehensively evaluate PathPocket, an evidence grounded multimodal agentic co-pilot designed to transform computational pathology workflows. Our results are structured around four core pillars. First, we construct a large-scale, evidence-graded multimodal pathology hypergraph, establishing a rigorous and traceable knowledge engine that forms the foundation of the system. Second, we rigorously evaluate PathPocket’s diagnostic reasoning capabilities across an exhaustive benchmark of 30 distinct clinical tasks. This benchmark spannes text-only clinical queries, multimodal ROI interpretations, and complex gigapixel WSI diagnostics, across which PathPocket consistently outperforms state-of-the-art large language and vision-language baselines. Third, detailed qualitative analyses demonstrate PathPocket’s ability to effectively mitigate AI hallucinations by providing transparent, evidence-backed diagnoses. The detailed findings for each of these pillars are presented in the following sections. 

2.1 Construction and characterization of the knowledge engine, a multimodal pathology hypergraph 

The foundation of PathPocket’s reasoning capabilities is a meticulously curated, evidence grounded knowledge base. To overcome the limitations of standard large language models that often hallucinate medical facts or lack verifiable traceability, we construct the most comprehensive multimodal pathology hypergraph assembled to date.

We initiate this pipeline by collating a large-scale corpus of 110,472 authorized medical documents. As shown in Figure 2A ,this corpus encompasses a broad spectrum of human anatomy, ensuring comprehensive coverage across specialized subfields (e.g., Central Nervous System, Gastrointestinal Tract, Respiratory System) as well as general pathological principles. To support evidence-based pathology reasoning, every document is rigorously tagged with an explicit evidence level based on an 8-tier adaptation of the GRAGE hierarchy ( Figure 2B ). To enable fine-grained evidence retrieval and traceable reasoning, we parse the corpus into 1,557,055 distinct semantic chunks. This conversion reveals a critical characteristic of medical knowledge density ( Figure 2C ): although individual case reports dominate the raw document count (64.4%), Textbooks and RCTs are highly dense, generating a disproportionately large-scale volume of semantic chunks (21.3% and 15.0%, respectively). From these graded semantic chunks, we extract a complex topology comprising 4.55 million entities and 7.10 million multi-entity relations. As illustrated in Figure 2D , multimodal entities“Image” and “Table” are integrated with text-based clinical entities such as “Pathological Finding”, “Diagnostic Method”, and “Disease.” These nodes are interconnected by clinically meaningful edges. The relation type distribution ( Figure 2E ) demonstrates a high density of actionable medical linkages, pre-dominantly featuring “AnatomicalInvolvement”, “HistologicCorrelation”, “PrognosisOutcome”, and “PathogenicMechanism”. Notably, unlike traditional graphs that are restricted to binary relations, a hypergraph naturally accommodates multivariate relationships by allowing a single hyperedge to connect an arbitrary number of entities. We present the distribution of hyper-edges based on their entity counts in Figure 2F . Out of the total 7,100,480 hyperedges, while binary relations account for a substantial portion (2,490,548), the vast majority of interactions involve three or more entities. Ternary relations (entity count =3) form the largest group with 3,427,467 hyperedges and a significant number of complex, higher-order relationships span across 4 to over 13 entities. This large-scale, graded, and multimodal hypergraph serves as the robust knowledge engine that directly powers PathPocket’s multi-agent retrieval and diagnostic reasoning. 

2.2 PathPocket excels in text-only clinical reasoning through evidence grounding 

To evaluate the fundamental pathology reasoning and pathology knowledge retrieval capabilities of PathPocket, we first benchmark our system on a comprehensive suite of 12 text-only pathology tasks. These tasks are carefully curated to encompass both standardized medical examinations and complex real-world clinical scenarios. The benchmark includes six public datasets derived from the United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE 1-3) and the China National Medical Licensing Examination (NMLE 2022-2024), alongside six private datasets originating from a large-scale real-world clinical cohort (SMU-Text 1-6). As illustrated in Figure 3A and 3B , the public datasets cover a broad spectrum of anatomical systems (predominantly gastrointestinal, respiratory, and female genital tracts) and diverse question types, with diagnosis and treatment being the most frequent. To ensure our evaluation reflects actual clinical practice, we assemble the private SMU-Text dataset comprising 1,854 real-world pathology cases. This private cohort is highly heterogeneous, featuring a balanced gender distribution, a wide age range (predominantly 41-60 years), a high prevalence of malignant cases (93.3%), and a comprehensive coverage of diverse anatomical systems ( Figure 3C ). We compare PathPocket against the state-of-the-art open-weight large language model series, Qwen3, scaling from 0.5 billion to 30 billion parameters. On the six public standardized examination datasets ( Figure 3D ), PathPocket demonstrates robust clinical knowledge, consistently achieving high accuracy across all tasks. Notably, PathPocket can exceed the performance of the largest baseline model, Qwen3-30B (e.g., 93.9% vs. 81.6% on USMLE 2). The advantages of PathPocket’s evidence-based agentic framework become even more pronounced on the six private, real-world clinical tasks ( Figure 3E ). Real-world pathology reports and queries are notoriously complex, often containing noisy, incomplete, or highly specialized information that challenges the parametric memory of standard LLMs. Across all six SMU-Text subsets, PathPocket consistently outperforms all Qwen3 baselines. For instance, on SMU-Text 5, PathPocket achieves an accuracy of 82.7%, significantly surpassing Qwen3-30B (72.8%, p < 0.05 ). Similar statistically significant improvements are observed in SMU-Text 1 ( p < 0.05 ) and SMU-Text 3 ( p < 0.05 ). These results compellingly demonstrate that integrating a rigorous, multimodal evidence hypergraph with a collaborative multi-agent reasoning framework enables PathPocket to resolve complex clinical queries with greater accuracy and reliability than scaling model parameters alone. 

2.3 PathPocket demonstrates superior multimodal reasoning on ROI-level tasks

To evaluate PathPocket’s capability in multimodal clinical reasoning, we construct a rigorous region-of-interest (ROI) level benchmark comprising 13 distinct visual-language tasks. This benchmark includes three widely used public datasets and ten challenging private tasks designed to closely mimic real-world pathology workflows. We first assess PathPocket on three public multimodal datasets: BreakHis (breast cancer), CCRCC (renal cell carcinoma), and Chaoyang (colon cancer). As shown in Figure 4A , we compare our system against the state-of-the-art Qwen3VL series (2B, 4B, and 8B parameters), as well as two specialized medical vision-language models, LLaVA-Med and Quilt-LLaVA. 2.4 PathPocket scales evidence-based reasoning to gigapixel whole-slide images 

While ROI evaluation demonstrates core multimodal capabilities, real-world pathology workflows require navigating and interpreting gigapixel whole-slide images (WSIs). WSI analysis remains challenging for conventional vision-language models owing to the scale of visual information and the need to integrate localized morphological features into a coherent diagnostic 

> 8/31

interpretation. To evaluate PathPocket’s utility at this scale, we curate a private dataset of 1,232 WSIs encompassing five demanding diagnostic tasks. As detailed in Figure 5A and 5B , this WSI cohort covers major oncology domains—gastric, colorectal, and breast cancers—and requires complex clinical determinations such as tumor grading, staging, and subtyping. Crucially, alongside a robust retrospective cohort, this dataset includes a prospective clinical subset ( Figure 5C ), enabling evaluation under prospective real-world clinical conditions. We benchmark PathPocket against the Qwen3VL series, LLaVA-Med, and Quilt-LLaVA across the five WSI-level tasks (Figure 5D ). PathPocket achieves an overall accuracy of 74.5%, substantially outperforming the strongest baseline model, Qwen3VL-8B (54.2%, p < 0.05 ). This superior performance is consistent across diverse specific tasks. For instance, in retrospective Gastric Cancer Grading and Colorectal Cancer (CRC) T-Staging, PathPocket achieves accuracies of 79.0% and 82.0%, significantly surpassing the Qwen3VL-8B model (72.7%, p < 0.05 and 76.7%, p < 0.05 , respectively). In the highly challenging task of Gastric Cancer Subtyping, PathPocket demonstrates robust generalization. On the retrospective subset, it outperformes the top baseline by a large margin (50.7% vs. 37.6%, p < 0.05 ). When evaluated on the prospective cohort, PathPocket maintains a high accuracy of 66.7%, whereas the performance of the Qwen3VL-8B model degraded to 52.8% (p < 0.05 ). Even in Breast Cancer Grading, a task characterized by high inter-observer variability, PathPocket maintains an accuracy of 70.5%. These results suggest an important advantage of evidence-guided multimodal reasoning: by utilizing its agentic patch-selection mechanism (as outlined in the system architecture) guided by an evidence grounded hypergraph, PathPocket effectively bridges the gap between massive WSI visual contexts and precise clinical decision-making, enabling more reliable reasoning over gigapixel pathology images than conventional parametric vision-language models. 

2.5 Qualitative analysis highlights the necessity of traceable evidence in complex diagnostics 

To understand the mechanics behind PathPocket’s superior quantitative performance and to evaluate its clinical interpretability, we conduct a qualitative analysis comparing its reasoning trajectories against baseline models. Standard large language and vision-language models rely exclusively on internal parametric memory, which frequently leads to confident hallucinations when confronted with complex pathology cases. PathPocket mitigates this by grounding its answers in retrieved, explicit medical evidence. 

Figure 6A illustrates a highly specific text-only diagnostic challenge involving a right atrial mass. The case provides a detailed histological description and a complex immunohistochemistry (IHC) panel. The standard Qwen3 model fails to synthesize this profile correctly; it overinterprets the negative S-100 result to rule out the correct diagnosis (atrial myxoma) and incorrectly associates the IHC profile with rhabdomyosarcoma. PathPocket, leveraging its evidence-retrieval agent, correctly identifies the tumor as an Atrial Myxoma. Furthermore, it performs a rigorous differential diagnosis by actively refuting rhabdomyosarcoma, noting that the latter strictly requires positive MyoD1 and Myogenin—markers absent in the case description. Most importantly, PathPocket appends traceable references (e.g., Diagnostic Atlas of Soft Tissue Tumor Pathology ), allowing the pathologist to instantly verify the rules applied. The advantages of evidence-grounding extend to multimodal tasks, as demonstrated in Figure 6B . In this case, a patient presents with a renal mass, accompanied by text describing PET-CT findings and ROI images showing distinct cellular morphology. The baseline Qwen3VL model superficially aligns the glandular structures with a benign “papillary adenoma” (Option E), failing to integrate the visual nuances with the tumor’s metabolic profile. PathPocket, however, accurately translates the visual data—identifying the specific combination of a “papillary structure” with “transparent or lightly stained cells”—and correlates this with the “mildly increased metabolism” noted in the text. It systematically rules out other malignant and benign differentials to arrive at the correct diagnosis of a Clear Cell Papillary Renal Cell Tumor. By explicitly citing top-tier evidence, including the 2023 CSCO Guidelines and classical textbooks like Heptinstall’s Renal Pathology , PathPocket provides traceable, evidence-supported diagnostic reasoning. These qualitative examples underscore that PathPocket does not merely act as an answer generator; it functions as an authentic clinical co-pilot, enhancing diagnostic safety and building user trust through rigorous, evidence-backed reasoning. 

## 3 Discussion 

In this study, we introduce PathPocket, an a multimodal agentic co-pilot for evidence grounded multimodal pathology reasoning. While AI has shown immense promise in medicine, its clinical adoption has been heavily limited by the “black-box” nature of large language and vision-language models, which are prone to hallucination and lack interpretability. PathPocket addresses this limitation by shifting the paradigm from parametric memory-based guessing to dynamic, verifiable evidence retrieval. By integrating a large-scale medical knowledge base with a collaborative reasoning framework, we have demonstrated a scalable pathway to evidence grounded computational pathology.

Traditional medical LLMs are typically trained on heterogeneous internet-scale corpora with variable evidence quality, leading to knowledge conflicts. In contrast, our knowledge base derives from a vast collection of over 110,000 public and authoritative documents spanning a wide array of sources, including clinical guidelines, randomized controlled trials, and classical textbooks. Every document was explicitly stratified using an 8-tier adaptation of the GRADE evidence hierarchy. By processing this immense volume of high-quality data, we constructe a large-scale multimodal pathology hypergraph containing over 4.55 million multimodal entities and 7.10 million relations. This unparalleled scale and strict evidence grading ensure that every diagnostic suggestion is backed by the clinically traceable evidence sources. Methodologically, PathPocket overcomes the limitations of monolithic neural networks through a highly dimensional multimodal hypergraph coupled with a collaborative multi-agent reasoning framework. Traditional knowledge graphs are fundamentally limited by flat, pairwise relationships. Our hypergraph architecture innovates by utilizing hyperedges to model complex, multivariate relationships. Moreover, this highly dimensional topology inherently supports incremental updating; new evidence can be continuously injected into the graph without the catastrophic forgetting or computationally prohibitive retraining associated with standard LLMs. Operating atop this robust hypergraph is our multi-agent framework. By decoupling the diagnostic workflow into specialized, interacting agents, the system efficiently forces the AI to generate transparent, step-by-step reasoning trajectories that approximate aspects of expert diagnostic workflows. The robustness and practical utility of our approach are rigorously validated through comprehensive evaluation. We evaluate PathPocket across an extensive benchmark of 30 distinct clinical tasks, ranging from text-only pathology questions to complex, multi-image ROI and WSI diagnostics derived from private hospital cohorts. Across all scales, PathPocket significantly outperforms state-of-the-art general and medical baselines. Despite the superior performance and interpretability demonstrated by PathPocket, its architectural complexity introduces higher computational overhead and inference latency than traditional single-turn LLMs. The system requires extra processing time to execute multi-hop retrieval trajectories across high-dimensional hyperedges and to orchestrate consensus among special-ized agents. However, this increased latency is highly acceptable in practical clinical workflows. Routine histopathological evaluations and complex WSI diagnostics typically operate on a turnaround scale of hours or days. Therefore, the few additional seconds required to generate transparent, verifiable reasoning trajectories represent a minor and justifiable trade-off for the critical gains in diagnostic rigor and hallucination mitigation. 

## 4 Method 

The methodology for developing and comprehensively validating the PathPocket framework is structured into six sequential components, moving from foundational data curation to system architecture and rigorous clinical evaluation. First, we detail the Collection and Stratification of Pathology Evidence, establishing a medically grounded knowledge repository wherein diverse literature is graded by its authoritative consensus level. To process this large-scale influx of multimodal data, we outline the Infrastructure and Tooling for Multi-Agent Operations, introducing the specialized vision-language foundation models, parsing engines, and database systems that serve as the “hands and eyes” of our framework. Leveraging these tools, we describe the Multimodal Pathology Hypergraph Construction via Multi-Agent Collaboration, detailing how autonomous agents systematically extract, verify, and topologically link complex clinical entities into a high-dimensional graph. For the inference phase, we present the Multi-Agent Retrieval-Augmented Pathology Reasoning pipeline, illustrating how specialized agents collaborate to parse noisy clinical queries, retrieve relevant hyperedges and multimodal patches, and synthesize evidence-graded diagnostic responses. To rigorously assess the diagnostic capabilities of this framework, we introduce a Comprehensive Pathology Evaluation Benchmark, which encompasses over 20,000 real-world evaluation items scaling from pure-text reasoning to gigapixel WSI diagnostics across multiple private hospital cohorts. Finally, we provide the Implementation Details, specifying the hardware configurations, model deployment strategies, and computational frameworks utilized throughout our experiments. 

4.1 Collection and Stratification of Pathology Evidence 

To establish a highly authoritative, hallucination-free knowledge engine for PathPocket, we curate the most comprehensive corpus of multimodal pathology evidence to date. The dataset comprises over 110,000 public and authorized medical documents (N = 110 , 472 ). To operationalize evidence-based medicine (EBM) within our AI framework, we systematically stratified all collected documents into an 8-tier evidence hierarchy, adapted from standard evidence-based medicine framework GRADE. The collection spans a diverse array of highly credible medical sources across the 8 evidence tiers: • Level 1: Guidelines ( n = 981 ). We aggregated gold-standard clinical and pathological guidelines from premier international and top-tier regional organizations, including the World Health Organization (WHO), American Joint

Committee on Cancer (AJCC), College of American Pathologists (CAP), NICE, ASCO, ESMO, Chinese Anti-Cancer Association (CACA), Chinese Society of Clinical Oncology (CSCO), and the National Health Commission (NHC). • Level 2: Systematic Reviews & Meta-Analyses ( n = 12 , 354 ). High-quality synthesis literature was systematically retrieved from PubMed Central (PMC). • Level 3: Randomized Controlled Trials ( n = 9, 305 ). Peer-reviewed clinical trials with rigorous controls were sourced from PMC. • Level 4: Observational & Cohort Studies ( n = 14 , 751 ). Including large-scale case-control and cohort studies, primarily extracted from PMC. • Level 5: Case Reports ( n = 71 , 176 ). 4.2 Infrastructure and Tooling for Multi-Agent Operations 

To empower the multi-agent workflow during both the hypergraph construction phase and the dynamic reasoning phase, PathPocket integrates a robust suite of state-of-the-art infrastructure and foundational models. These specialized tools function as the “hands and eyes” of the LLM agents, enabling them to process complex multimodal data, execute efficient retrievals, and handle gigapixel images.  

> High-Accuracy Document Parsing.

During the initial stage of hypergraph construction, the Parsing Agent utilizes MinerU 29 ,an accurate layout-aware document parsing engine. Medical textbooks and clinical guidelines are notoriously complex, containing nested tables, multi-column layouts, and interwoven figures. MinerU structurally decomposes these PDFs, precisely isolating text blocks, hierarchical headers, and multimodal elements (images/tables) while preserving their reading order and structural semantics, providing a clean data stream for subsequent LLM extraction.  

> Hypergraph Storage and Topological Querying.

The hypergraph is stored and queried using PostgreSQL 30 , augmented with the pgvector extension. Rather than relying on specialized but rigid graph databases, we leverage PostgreSQL’s robust array operations to model hyperedges. For instance, the Retrieval Agent dynamically generates SQL commands utilizing array overlap operators (e.g., WHERE entities && $2::text[] ) to rapidly fetch hyperedges that intersect with the queried clinical entities, ordering the results by the degree of entity overlap. This ensures mathematically rigorous topological traversal alongside semantic vector search.  

> WSI Patch Selection.

WSIs are gigapixel-scale and cannot be directly ingested by standard MLLMs. To bridge this gap during the reasoning phase, the Query Parsing Agent employs TITAN 31 as its visual attention tool. TITAN computes an attention heatmap across the entire WSI to identify tissue regions with the highest diagnostic salience. It then autonomously crops and selects the top representative patches, transforming a computationally intractable WSI into a condensed set of highly informative ROIs.  

> Multimodal Embedding Foundations

To map all textual and visual data into high-dimensional vector space for cross-modal semantic retrieval, our system relies on two specialized foundation models. Text Embedding (BGE-m3 32 ): Utilized by the Consolidation Agent to generate dense vector representations for raw text chunks, entity attributes, and hyperedge descriptions. Its multilingual capabilities ensure robust alignment across diverse medical nomenclatures. Vision Embedding (Virchow2 33 ): 

A pathology-specific vision foundation model utilized to encode all visual modalities. During hypergraph construction, it embeds parsed textbook figures; during inference, it embeds the patient’s WSI ROIs selected by TITAN. This enables the Retrieval Agent to perform highly accurate image-to-image and text-to-image semantic matching.  

> 12/31 Semantic Context Filtering.

Given the large-scale scale of the evidence retrieved from the hypergraph, the Filtering Agent employs Qwen3-Reranker 34 as its primary scoring engine. It analyzes the semantic interplay between the structured query intent and the retrieved evidence chunks. By providing highly calibrated relevance scores, Qwen3-Reranker allows the Filtering Agent to confidently discard noise and truncate the context window, ensuring that the Diagnosis Agent is fed only the most pertinent and high-quality evidence. 

4.3 Multimodal Pathology Hypergraph Construction via Multi-Agent Collaboration 

We conceptualize the construction of the multimodal pathology hypergraph as a collaborative multi-agent workflow. Over pathology documents, four specialized agents operate sequentially: (i) a Parsing and Chunking Agent for data structuralization, (ii) an Extraction Agent for initial entity and n-ary relation generation, (iii) a Rectifying Agent for refinement, and (iv) a Consolidation and Indexing Agent for topological assembly and multimodal dense embedding. Let D denote the set of source documents. Each document is split into a set of text chunks C txt = {c1, . . . , cN } and multimodal chunks C mm = {u1, . . . , uM } (images/tables). A chunk x carries content sx, provenance, and identifier id (x). > Diagnosis Agent

Finally, the Diagnosis Agent Adiag synthesizes the clinical narrative and formulates the final answer. Within the generation prompt Pdiag , the filtered context E is meticulously formatted. The Diagnosis Agent is explicitly instructed to weigh these tiers during its reasoning process—prioritizing high-tier consensus over lower-tier observational reports when resolving conflicting information. The agent then deduces the diagnosis strictly from this graded context, explicitly acknowledges insufficient information if required to prevent hallucination, and appends structured reference citations for total transparency. 

4.5 Comprehensive Pathology Evaluation Benchmark 

To rigorously validate the diagnostic capabilities and clinical utility of PathPocket, we construct a comprehensive pathology evaluation benchmark comprising over 20,000 rigorous evaluation items. While existing computational pathology studies frequently overfit to highly curated, publicly available datasets, our benchmark is heavily anchored in private, real-world clinical data sourced directly from hospital workflows. The benchmark is structurally divided into three categories of escalating clinical complexity: text-only reasoning, ROI-level multimodal interpretation, and WSI-level diagnostics. 

> Text-Only Tasks

This foundational tier evaluates the system’s medical reasoning and natural language understanding capabilities in the absence of visual context. 

USMLE 1-3. The USMLE subset comprises 133 English-language clinical vignettes formatted similarly to the United States Medical Licensing Examination. Designed to isolate general clinical reasoning under standard prompt distributions, these multiple-choice items span mixed internal medicine systems without being restricted to a single organ. 

NMLE 1-3. The NMLE dataset consists of 142 Chinese-language cases aligned with the style of the National Medical Licensing Examination of China from the nominal years 2022 to 2024. It provides general clinical coverage and evaluates foundational medical knowledge using standard single-best-answer multiple-choice prompts against fixed evaluation keys. 

SMU-Text 1-6. Qwen3. Qwen3 38 is Alibaba Cloud’s flagship large language model series, offering a comprehensive suite of dense and Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) architectures ranging from 0.6B to 235B parameters. Qwen3-VL. The model demonstrates exceptional capability in document parsing and video temporal understanding, supported by its omnidocument processing framework that handles complex layouts, tables, and mathematical formulas. While not specifically optimized for medical applications, Qwen3-VL’s robust multilingual support and object grounding capabilities make it suitable for preliminary medical image analysis tasks, particularly in resource-constrained settings.

4.6 Implementation Details 

The framework of PathPocket is implemented in PyTorch 42 . To ensure low latency during real-time retrieval and gener-ation, a critical requirement for clinical workflows, all LLMs driving the specialized agents are deployed using vLLM (https://github.com/vllm-project/vllm ), a high-throughput and memory-efficient inference and serving engine. All computational experiments, including hypergraph construction, dense embedding generation, and multi-agent inference, are conducted on a high-performance on-premise computing cluster equipped with 8 × NVIDIA H800 GPUs (80GB VRAM per card). The specific foundational tools and models integrated into the multi-agent system are sourced from their official open-source repositories. MinerU: Utilized by the Parsing Agent for layout-aware PDF structuralization and data extrac-tion from pathology textbooks ( https://github.com/opendatalab/MinerU ). TITAN: Employed for attention-based ROIs selection from gigapixel WSIs ( https://github.com/mahmoodlab/TITAN ). Virchow2: The pathology-specific vision foundation model used for embedding multimodal nodes and visual ROIs ( https://huggingface.co/ paige-ai/Virchow2 ). BGE-m3: The state-of-the-art multilingual text encoder utilized for computing dense vector repre-sentations of text chunks and hyperedges ( https://github.com/FlagOpen/FlagEmbedding ). Qwen3-Reranker: The LLM-based model utilized by the Filtering Agent to deeply evaluate semantic relevance and truncate context noise (https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-Reranker-8B ).
