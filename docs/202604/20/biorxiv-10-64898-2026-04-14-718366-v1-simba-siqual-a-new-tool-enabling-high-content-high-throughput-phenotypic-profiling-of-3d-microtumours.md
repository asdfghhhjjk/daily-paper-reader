## 摘要
三维微型肿瘤模型（如球状体）在癌症研究中日益广泛应用，因为与传统的二维培养相比，这些模型更能真实反映肿瘤的结构、增长和侵袭特征。然而，用于定量分析的稳健且易用的工具仍然有限。本研究介绍了 SImBA-SiQuAl，这是一种用于三维球状体和类器官高通量定量表型分析的集成开源工作流程。该流程将 SImBA（一个用于自动化图像分析的框架，能够高效执行质量控制的图像分割及多特征提取）与 SiQuAl（一个自动执行全面统计和多变量分析的下游分析平台）相结合，从而揭示不同实验条件下的表型差异。在首个案例研究中，SImBA-SiQuAl 成功解析了不同癌细胞系间的内在侵袭表型；在第二个案例中，该工作流程量化了药物筛选球状体实验中均一和异质性的反应。总体而言，SImBA-SiQuAl 为癌症研究中的高通量、高内涵微型肿瘤表型组学提供了一种及时的新工具。

研究动机：三维微型肿瘤测定（如球状体和类器官）在临床前研究中应用日益增多。这些测定生成丰富的表型成像数据，但定量的自动化分析仍是主要瓶颈。这限制了可重复性、可扩展性以及大规模高内涵表型组学研究的广泛采用，同时意味着在干扰研究等场景中，生物学上具有意义的（异质性）表型反应可能无法得到全面解析。SImBA-SiQuAl 的开发旨在通过提供一个在图像处理和下游分析方面均有解决方案的开源集成工作流程来弥补这一空白。这使得跨实验条件进行三维微型肿瘤表型的深入定量分析成为可能。

研究亮点：SImBA-SiQuAl 提供了一个完整的端到端工作流程，用于高通量、高内涵、定量的三维微型肿瘤分析——从质量控制的图像分割到统计、多变量和基于聚类的生物学解释。SImBA-SiQuAl 可广泛应用于多种三维系统和测定类型。我们展示了该工作流程能够在大规模下捕捉具有生物学意义的异质性和处理反应，从而支持稳健、无偏的分析。通过结合易用性、灵活性与分析深度，SImBA-SiQuAl 满足了三维临床前研究中对先进开源分析工具的迫切需求。

## Abstract
Three-dimensional microtumour models such as spheroids are increasingly used in cancer research as they better capture tumour architecture, growth and invasion than conventional two-dimensional cultures. However, robust and accessible tools for quantitative analysis remain limited. Here we present SImBA-SiQuAl, an integrated open-source workflow for high-throughput quantitative phenotyping of 3D spheroids and organoids. The pipeline combines SImBA, an automated image-analysis framework for performant quality-controlled image segmentation and multi-feature extraction from spheroid assays, with SiQuAl, a downstream analysis platform that automatically performs comprehensive statistical and multivariate analyses to reveal phenotypic differences between experimental conditions. In a first case study, SImBA-SiQuAl resolves intrinsic invasion phenotypes between cancer cell lines. In a second case study, the workflow quantifies both uniform and heterogeneous responses in a spheroid drug screening assay. Together, SImBA-SiQuAl provides a new, timely tool for high-throughput, high-content microtumour phenomics in cancer research.

MOTIVATION3D-microtumour assays such as spheroids and organoids are increasingly used in preclinical research. These assays generate rich phenotypic imaging data, but quantitative automated analysis remains a major bottleneck. This limits reproducibility, scalability, and broad adoption for large-scale, high-content phenomics studies, but also implies biologically relevant phenotypic (heterogeneous) responses in e.g. perturbation studies may not be comprehensively addressed. SImBA-SiQuAl is developed to address this gap by providing an open-source, integrated workflow offering solutions in both the image processing and downstream analysis. Together, this enables in-depth quantitative analysis of 3D microtumour phenotypes across experimental settings.
